PART 1 - BASIC FORMATTING

The basic formatting for what I call "TONES" is this.

Phonemes aren't as hard as you think.

They are formatted like this.

An example of what it looks like is this

[diy<200,26>strr<150,28>biy<500,36>ng<300,34>thuh<350> piy<500,33>s]


PART 2 - TONES

Tones are basic.

They only have 2 parts.

The first part is the tone in hz.

The 2nd part is the tone's length.

Since dectalk only has one track what you need to do to play chords is play 3 or more notes really fast, one after another.

An example of this technique being used is this

[:t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15 :t698 15 :t880 15 :t1046 15]

PART 3 - PHONEMES

Phonemes are a bit complex, not nessicarly in the fact that it's difficult to use but there's so many more variables and combinations of them.

An example of this is

[hxiy wuhs ey jhaaliy gurd fehllow]

for the sentence, "he was a jolly good fellow."

A good resource I recommend is this as it has a good list of all of the phonemes you can use.
